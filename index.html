About Me

I am Nguyễn Minh Hiếu, a passionate researcher in computational physics. I graduated from VNU University of Science, majoring in Computational Physics. My thesis was on "Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells", where I computed the structure of ethylene fluorine ethylene (C2H4)n membranes. I am highly interested in atomic structure simulations and molecular structures.

I've also participated in molecular structure simulation tutorials led by Professor Jer-Lai Kuo and later worked as a Research Assistant in his lab at the Institute of Atomic and Molecular Sciences, Academia Sinica, Taiwan.

My Research

Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells

My undergraduate research focused on building and calculating the structure of (C2H4)n membranes, crucial in fuel cell development.

Atomic Structure Simulations

Simulating atomic structures with advanced computational methods is one of my primary interests, helping to understand complex molecular systems.

Molecular Dynamics

Using computational tools, I explore the dynamic properties of molecules and their behavior under various physical conditions.
